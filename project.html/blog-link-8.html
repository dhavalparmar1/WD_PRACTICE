Understanding SSL Security: What You Need to Know

FAQ

January 7, 2023 - 4 minutes read

SSL for Website Security

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a technology that establishes a secure, encrypted connection between a web server and a client (such as a web browser). It is used to protect the sensitive information that is transmitted between the two, such as passwords, credit card numbers, and personal details.

Benefits of using SSL for your Website

SSL works by using a combination of public and private keys to encrypt and decrypt the data that is being transmitted. When a client establishes a connection with a server using SSL, the server sends the client its SSL certificate, which contains the server’s public key. The client then uses this public key to encrypt the data it sends to the server, and the server uses its private key to decrypt it.

One of the main benefits of SSL is that it helps to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks. In a man-in-the-middle attack, an attacker intercepts the communication between the client and the server and attempts to steal or alter the sensitive information being transmitted. With SSL, the attacker would not be able to decrypt the data, as they do not have the private key needed to do so.

Another benefit of SSL is that it helps to establish trust between the client and the server. When a client connects to a server using SSL, the client can be assured that it is communicating with the legitimate server and not an imposter. This is because the SSL certificate issued by the server is signed by a trusted third party, known as a certificate authority (CA).

Types of SSL Certificates

There are different levels of SSL security, and the level of security provided depends on the type of SSL certificate used. The most common types of SSL certificates are:

Domain Validated (DV) certificates: These are the most basic type of SSL certificate, and they are issued after the CA verifies that the applicant owns the domain for which the certificate is being issued.

Organization Validated (OV) certificates: These certificates require more extensive validation than DV certificates. In addition to verifying that the applicant owns the domain, the CA also verifies the applicant’s organization information.

Extended Validation (EV) certificates: These are the most secure type of SSL certificate, and they require the most extensive validation process. In addition to verifying domain ownership and organization information, the CA also verifies the legal, physical, and operational existence of the organization.

How to get SSL Certificate for your Website?

There are several steps you can follow to get an SSL certificate for your website: Choose an SSL certificate provider: There are many companies that offer SSL certificates, and the provider you choose will depend on your specific needs and budget. Some popular SSL certificate providers include Comodo, Symantec, and GoDaddy.

Choose the type of SSL certificate you need: There are different types of SSL certificates, and the one you choose will depend on your specific needs. The most common types are Domain Validated (DV) certificates, Organization Validated (OV) certificates, and Extended Validation (EV) certificates. DV certificates are the most basic and are issued after the certificate authority (CA) verifies that the applicant owns the domain for which the certificate is being issued. OV certificates require more extensive validation, including verification of the applicant’s organization information. EV certificates are the most secure and require the most extensive validation process, including verification of the legal, physical, and operational existence of the organization.

Generate a certificate signing request (CSR): A CSR is a file that contains your website’s information and is used to apply for an SSL certificate. To generate a CSR, you will need to use a tool such as OpenSSL or a tool provided by your web hosting provider.

Submit your CSR and other required documentation: Once you have generated your CSR, you will need to submit it to the SSL certificate provider along with any other required documentation. This may include proof of ownership of the domain for which the certificate is being issued and proof of your organization’s existence (for OV and EV certificates).

Wait for the SSL certificate to be issued: After you have submitted your CSR and other required documentation, the SSL certificate provider will review your application and issue the SSL certificate if everything is in order. This process can take a few days to a week, depending on the provider and the type of certificate you are applying for.

Install the SSL certificate: Once you have received your SSL certificate, you will need to install it on your web server. The process for doing this will depend on your specific server and the type of certificate you are using. You can typically find instructions for installing an SSL certificate on your server in the documentation provided by the SSL certificate provider.

In conclusion, SSL security is a technology that helps to establish a secure, encrypted connection between a web server and a client, and it helps to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks and establish trust between the two parties. There are different levels of SSL security, and the level of security provided depends on the type of SSL certificate used.
